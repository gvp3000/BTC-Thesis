SOFR Spread

Fed Funds: 4.25–4.50%  ·  SOFR: ~4.30%
Trend: Stable — no stress in overnight lending markets
SOFR (Secured Overnight Financing Rate) is the rate banks charge each other to borrow cash overnight. The spread between SOFR and the Fed Funds rate is your early-warning signal for banking system stress.

Why it matters: When this spread exceeds 25bp, banks are running short on reserves — the same condition that triggered the 2019 repo crisis, when the Fed had to inject $75B within 24 hours. That forced injection is what starts your thesis clock.

Current context: At ~2bp the market is calm. But with RRP near zero, there is almost no buffer left. A large Treasury auction or quarter-end funding crunch could push this into stress territory without warning. Check weekly.
Alert ladder: normal → mild → stress → trigger → crisis →

The RRP facility is a parking lot for excess cash in the financial system. Money market funds and banks park cash here when they have nowhere better to put it. As this drains to zero, it means excess reserves have been fully absorbed — the system is at maximum tightness.

Why critical: This is not a warning — this is the precondition already met. At $28B we are functionally at zero. In 2019, the repo market seized when a similar condition hit without warning. The SOFR spike is not an if, it is a when.

Zero-date projections: Bull case June 2026. Base case Sept 2026. May floor permanently at $10–20B.

CInflation Ceiling — Core PCE~2.8% — OK
The Fed's dual mandate (employment + price stability) constrains aggressive balance sheet expansion when inflation is elevated. Below 2.5%: full freedom to expand. 2.5–3.0%: moderate room (current). 3.0–3.5%: constrained, expect smaller response. Above 3.5% for 3+ months: structurally blocked — reduce position 50%.

Why 3.5% specifically: historically the Fed has tolerated expansion between 2.5–3.0% (2010–2014, early 2020–2021). Above 3.5% sustained, Congressional and political pressure makes full QE nearly untenable. They'd do the minimum — not the thesis.

Check FRED: PCEPILFE, published last Friday of each month by BEA.
⚡ Kill trigger: Core PCE above 3.5% for 3 consecutive monthly readings → reduce 50%
