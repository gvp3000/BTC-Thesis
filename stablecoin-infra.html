Multiplier 1: Stablecoin supply growth. Supply goes from $310B → $1T. That's a 3.2× increase in the base that generates Curve's volume and fees. This projection comes from 21Shares, JPMorgan, and Visa — not crypto Twitter.

Multiplier 2: Fee share growth. Curve's Ethereum DEX fee share went from 1.6% to 44% in one year. If Curve maintains or grows share as stablecoins scale (likely — its StableSwap algorithm is purpose-built for this), fee share provides an additional multiplier on top of supply growth.

These multiply, they don't add. If stablecoin supply 3×'s and Curve's volume share grows even modestly, the revenue growth is geometric, not linear. The token price should reprice to reflect the new revenue reality — and it hasn't even begun to do that yet.
