2026 Trade Thesis

Why I expect monetary expansion before the November 2026 midterms, what I'm watching to confirm it, and the conditions that kill the trade.

Updated Feb 21, 2026 · v1

For seven independent reasons, I expect net monetary expansion in the United States over the next ten months. Bitcoin, as a fixed-supply asset with 91% historical correlation to money supply growth, captures that expansion with a roughly 90-day lag. The thesis doesn't require all seven forces to fire — just enough to produce net expansion within the window.

The specific mechanism — rate cuts, balance sheet expansion, fiscal spending, or all three — matters less than the structural reality: the incentive to expand is overwhelming and the constraints against it are weakening.

Key Takeaways
1. Seven independent forces push toward U.S. monetary expansion — three confirmed, two probable, two uncertain — all at the same time.
2. The mechanism chain: SOFR spike → Fed injection → WALCL rises → M2 grows → BTC responds (~90 day lag). Any broken link kills the trade.
3. Core PCE above 3.5% for 3+ months is the most likely failure mode — the Fed can't expand into persistent inflation.

Where it breaks: In 2024–25, BTC outran net liquidity — ETF demand, halving cycle, and institutional adoption created a separate demand layer. The correlation isn't 1:1 in real time. It's directional over quarters: when net liquidity contracts, BTC eventually corrects. When it expands, BTC eventually rallies. The lag and magnitude vary. Treat this as a regime indicator, not a price predictor.

Seven Arguments for Expansion
01 Confirmed
QT Ended — Headwind Gone
Fed ended quantitative tightening Dec 2025. Balance sheet flat at ~$6.6T. Every force below pushes against zero resistance instead of fighting a strong current.
Updated Dec 2025 → FLAT (no longer shrinking)
02 Confirmed
Liquidity Buffer Depleted
RRP near zero after holding $2.5T at peak. Next time overnight funding tightens, the Fed must inject directly. No vote required. 2019 repo crisis conditions.
Updated Feb 2026 ↓ RRP at ~$0 (buffer gone)
03 Probable
FOMC Projects Three More Cuts
Fed Funds at 3.50–3.75% after three 2025 cuts. Bowman's January speech projects three more. Each 25bp cut = ~$40B credit capacity. Three cuts = ~$120B into banking.
Updated Jan 2026 → ON HOLD (watching inflation)
04 Confirmed
Fiscal Spending Already Law
OBBBA authorized $135–140B flowing into 2026. CBO projects $1.9–2.1T deficit. Debt ceiling suspended through January 2027. No new legislation needed.
Updated Jan 2026 ↑ DEPLOYING (funds flowing)
05 Probable
Midterms Force Loose Conditions
November 2026 is the deadline. K-shaped economy — asset owners thriving, wage earners squeezed — amplifies the incentive. The political cure for that pain is the expansion catalyst.
Updated Feb 2026 ⏳ 9 MONTHS to midterms
06 Uncertain
Warsh Fed Transition
Kevin Warsh nominated Jan 30, 2026. First chair meeting ~June. Stephen Miran pushing for cuts from inside. Amplifier, not the engine.
Updated Jan 2026 ⏳ PENDING (confirmation)
07 Uncertain
AI CapEx + Global Liquidity Tailwinds
~$750B in hyperscaler AI spending multiplies through the real economy. Global M2 has surpassed $123T. ECB cutting, China stimulating. BTC is a global asset. These are tailwinds, not drivers — but they push the same direction.
Updated Feb 2026 ↑ SPENDING (CapEx accelerating)

Three confirmed, two probable, two uncertain — all pushing the same direction at the same time. The question isn't whether expansion happens, but whether it's fast enough and large enough within the window.
