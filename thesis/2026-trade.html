2026 Trade Thesis

Why I expect monetary expansion before the November 2026 midterms, what I'm watching to confirm it, and the conditions that kill the trade.

Updated Feb 21, 2026 · v1

For seven independent reasons, I expect net monetary expansion in the United States over the next ten months. Bitcoin, as a fixed-supply asset with 91% historical correlation to money supply growth, captures that expansion with a roughly 90-day lag. The thesis doesn't require all seven forces to fire — just enough to produce net expansion within the window.

The specific mechanism — rate cuts, balance sheet expansion, fiscal spending, or all three — matters less than the structural reality: the incentive to expand is overwhelming and the constraints against it are weakening.

Key Takeaways
1. Seven independent forces push toward U.S. monetary expansion — three confirmed, two probable, two uncertain — all at the same time.
2. The mechanism chain: SOFR spike → Fed injection → WALCL rises → M2 grows → BTC responds (~90 day lag). Any broken link kills the trade.
3. Core PCE above 3.5% for 3+ months is the most likely failure mode — the Fed can't expand into persistent inflation.

Geopolitical Catalysts

These aren't independent expansion arguments — they're obstacle removers. They don't cause liquidity expansion directly, but they widen the Fed's runway to ease by lowering inflation pressure and reducing uncertainty. If both land, the 7 arguments above get significantly easier to execute.

Trade In Negotiation
U.S.–China Trade Deal
Reduced tariffs = lower import costs = lower CPI pressure = more room for the Fed to cut rates. A deal would also reduce supply chain uncertainty, boosting corporate confidence and investment.
Mechanism: Tariff reduction → import prices fall → CPI eases → Core PCE drops → Fed has room to cut → credit expansion → BTC benefits.
Timeline
2025Tariff escalation drove inflation higher, limiting Fed flexibility
Q1 2026Negotiations reported active. No framework announced
ImpactA deal could reduce Core PCE by 0.3–0.5% — potentially the difference between "hold" and "cut"
TODO: Monitor trade representative statements, tariff schedule changes, and bilateral meeting announcements. Any framework deal = upgrade rate cut probability.
Conflict In Negotiation
Ukraine Peace Agreement
Peace stabilizes energy prices (oil + natural gas), reduces European fiscal strain on defense, and unwinds the geopolitical risk premium in global markets. Capital returns to risk assets.
Mechanism: Ceasefire → energy price stabilization → European inflation eases → ECB eases further → global M2 accelerates → risk appetite returns → BTC benefits as global asset.
Timeline
Feb 2022Invasion began. Energy crisis → European inflation → ECB forced to tighten
Q1 2026Peace negotiations reported. Framework uncertain
ImpactPeace removes geopolitical risk premium and frees European capital for risk assets. Amplifies global liquidity tailwind (#7)
TODO: Track ceasefire announcements, energy futures (Brent, TTF natural gas), and European defense spending signals. Peace = upgrade argument #7 confidence.

Neither catalyst is required for the thesis to work. But if both land, they solve the biggest risk — inflation staying too high for the Fed to ease. A China deal attacks CPI from the import side. Ukraine peace attacks it from the energy side. Together they could drop Core PCE below 2.5%, giving the Fed a wide open runway.
