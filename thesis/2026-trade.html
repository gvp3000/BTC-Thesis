2026 Trade Thesis

Why I believe monetary expansion is structurally likely before November 2026, what I'm watching to confirm it, and the conditions that kill the trade.

Updated February 21, 2026

The argument

For seven independent reasons, I expect net monetary expansion in the United States over the next ten months. Bitcoin, as a fixed-supply asset with 91% historical correlation to money supply growth, captures that expansion with a roughly 90-day lag. The thesis doesn't require all seven forces to fire — just enough to produce net expansion within the window.

The specific mechanism — rate cuts, balance sheet expansion, fiscal spending, or all three — matters less than the structural reality: the incentive to expand is overwhelming and the constraints against it are weakening.

Seven reasons to expect expansion

01 QT ended — the headwind is gone

The Fed ended quantitative tightening on December 1, 2025, stopping a two-year drain of ~$95B/month. The balance sheet is now flat at ~$6.6T. This alone isn't expansion, but it means every force below pushes against zero resistance instead of fighting a strong current.

Confirmed
02 The Fed's liquidity buffer is depleted

The Reverse Repo Facility — which held $2.5T at peak — is near zero. This is the most mechanical signal in the thesis. With no buffer, the next time overnight funding tightens, the Fed must inject directly. No vote required. The 2019 repo crisis happened under the same conditions.

Confirmed
03 FOMC projects three more rate cuts this year

After three cuts in 2025, Fed Funds sit at 3.50–3.75%. Vice Chair Bowman's January speech included three more in her projections, citing labor market fragility. Each 25bp cut creates ~$40B in credit capacity. Three cuts = ~$120B flowing through the banking system.

Probable
04 Massive fiscal spending is already law

The One Big Beautiful Bill Act authorized $135–140B in stimulus rolling into 2026, with the CBO projecting a $1.9–2.1T deficit for the year. The debt ceiling is suspended through January 2027. This money doesn't need new legislation — it's already authorized and flowing.

Confirmed
05 Midterm elections force loose conditions

November 2026 is the deadline. Presidents historically engineer loose monetary conditions 12–18 months before midterms. The K-shaped economy — asset owners thriving, wage earners squeezed — amplifies the incentive. The political cure for that pain is the expansion catalyst.

Probable
06 The Warsh Fed transition tilts dovish at mid-year

Kevin Warsh was nominated as Fed Chair on January 30, 2026 — first meeting as chair would be ~June. Stephen Miran is already pushing for cuts from inside. The thesis doesn't require Warsh to be aggressively dovish. He's an amplifier, not the engine.

Uncertain
07 AI CapEx and global liquidity provide tailwinds

~$750B in hyperscaler AI spending multiplies through the real economy. Global M2 has surpassed $111T. The ECB is cutting, China is stimulating. BTC is a global asset. These are tailwinds, not drivers — but they push the same direction.

Uncertain

Three confirmed, two probable, two uncertain — all pushing the same direction at the same time. The question isn't whether expansion happens, but whether it's fast enough and large enough within the window.

Key metrics to track

Every metric maps to one link in a mechanism chain: SOFR spikes → Fed injects → WALCL rises → M2 grows → BTC responds (~90 days). If any link fails, the chain breaks there.

Weekly — Thursdays after 4:30pm ET

WALCL — Fed Total Assets

The single most important number. Rising 4+ consecutive weeks after a Fed injection = sustained expansion confirmed. Rising 1–2 weeks then flat = sterilized injection, chain broke.

Currently ~$6.6T, flat since QT ended.

→ FRED WALCL
SOFR — Overnight Funding Rate

The trigger signal. Sustained spike >25bp above the Fed Funds ceiling = the Fed has lost rate control, forcing immediate injection. Currently in normal range at ~3.65–3.73%.

→ NY Fed SOFR
RRP — Liquidity Buffer

How much cushion exists before a SOFR spike becomes unavoidable. Near zero = maximum tightness.

→ FRED RRPONTSYD
TGA — Treasury Spending Account

When Treasury spends this balance down, it injects money into banking directly — stealth QE. OBBBA spending flows through here.

→ FRED WTREGEN

Monthly — Expansion confirmation

M2 — Money Supply

The transmission signal. If WALCL is rising but M2 isn't, banks aren't lending — the chain is broken. M2 acceleration is the prerequisite for the ~90-day BTC lag. Currently ~$22.3T, at all-time highs with slow growth.

→ FRED M2SL
Core PCE — Inflation Ceiling

Determines how much room the Fed has to expand. Below 2.5% = full room. Above 3.5% for 3+ months = the Fed is structurally blocked. This is the most likely failure mode. Currently ~3% headline, but underlying ex-tariffs closer to 2% per Bowman's January assessment.

→ FRED PCEPILFE
Weekly workflow (15 min, Thursdays): Check WALCL, SOFR, RRP after 4:30pm. Monthly, check core PCE on the last Friday. If nothing's changed, close the tab.

What kills the thesis

A thesis without invalidation conditions is a religion. These rules were written while calm. Refer to them when you're not. Full interactive checklist on the Invalidation Tracker →

Inflation ceiling. Core PCE above 3.5% for 3+ consecutive months. The Fed can't expand aggressively while inflation is running hot — they'll inject the minimum and stop.

→ Reduce position 50%, lower targets

Sterilized injection. WALCL flat or falling over 4+ weeks after a confirmed Fed injection. The Fed fixed the immediate problem but didn't follow with sustained expansion. Chain broke.

→ Re-evaluate, reduce if other forces insufficient

Time horizon. Catalysts haven't materialized by your defined deadline. The 2026 trade has a finite window — even if the long-term debt thesis survives, this trade is dead.

→ Define your date, define your action

Black swans. Stablecoin collapse (USDT/USDC under $0.90), spot BTC ETF suspension, US sovereign credit event, or critical Bitcoin network vulnerability.

→ 100% exit, sell first, analyze later

Solvency floor. If income can't cover obligations while the position is underwater, you're a forced seller at the worst possible time.

→ Confirm you can survive position going to zero
